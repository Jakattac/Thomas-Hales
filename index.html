Thomas Hales

Thomas Hales was an influential Irish politician who played a significant role in Irelands history.

Early Years

Thomas Hales was born at Knocknacurra, Ballinadee, near Bandon on a family farm owned by his father, Robert Hales, an activist in the Irish Land War and a reputed member of the Irish Republican Brotherhood and his wife, Margaret.

Hales was the sixth of nine children (five sons and four daughters). He was educated at Ballinadee national school and Warner's Lane school, Bandon. After leaving school he worked at Harte's timber yard, Bandon.

Irish Independence

During the Irish War of Independence, Thomas Hales joined the Irish Volunteers and actively participated in the fight for independence.
